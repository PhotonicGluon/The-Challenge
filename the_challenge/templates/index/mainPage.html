Information About The Challenge

There will be 14 questions in The Challenge. This means that you will have an average of approximately 64.3 seconds to solve each question in the 15 minutes (900 seconds) given to you.

However, expect that the questions' difficulty to increase as you progress.

During the 15 minutes, all four movements of Edvard Grieg's Peer Gynt Suite No. 1, Op. 46 will be played.

The following are the 14 questions' testing points:

  1. Addition, subtraction, multiplication or division of two small numbers.
  2. Harder addition, subtraction, multiplication, and/or division question.
  3. Simple "solving for $x$" problem. Note that $x$ is in a linear equation.
  4. Determine values of $a$, $b$ and $c$ in the expression $y = a \sin\left(\frac xb\right) + c$ or $y = a \cos\left(\frac xb\right) + c$ when given a graph of that function.
  5. Application of the Polynomial Remainder Theorem on a given polynomial.
  6. Identify, using the discriminant, which of the three given quadratic equations is possible to produce real roots. Also tests on solving quadratic equations. It is guaranteed that both roots of the quadratic equation with real roots are unique.
  7. Determine the $r$th term of a binomial expansion. It is guaranteed that the value of $n$ in $\left(ax^p \pm \frac{c}{dx^q}\right)^n$ will not exceed 8.
  8. Solve an equation involving two nested surds.
  9. Solving of a logarithm-cum-modulus equation.
  10. Derivative of $\sin \left(ax + b\right)$, $\cos \left(ax + b\right)$, $\tan \left(ax + b\right)$, $e^{ax + b}$, and $\ln \left(ax + b\right)$, together with sums and differences.
  11. Definite integral of a polynomial. It is guaranteed that the degree of the polynomial will not exceed 5.
  12. Integral of $\left(ax + b\right)^n$ for any rational $n$ (including negative $n$, excluding the case of $n = -1$), $\sin \left(ax + b\right)$, $\cos \left(ax + b\right)$, $\sec^2x$ and/or $e^{ax + b}$, together with constant multiples, sums and differences.
  13. Combination of derivatives and solving of cubic equation for $x$. It is guaranteed that there will be at least one trivial solution (i.e. 1, 2, -1 or -2), all solutions are integers, and all three roots of the cubic equation are unique.
  14. Simultaneous equations involving a linear equation (a chord of a circle) and the equation of that circle. It is guaranteed that there are two unique solutions of $x$.

Some practice questions are provided for your reference, along with their answers. You are encouraged to look at them before attempting The Challenge.

Information for Participants

  • Do not reload the page when playing The Challenge.
  • All answers are to be typed in the MathLex language, as specified here. The following are several important pointers when entering your answer:
    • To enter a number like "one million, two hundred and thirty-four thousand, five hundred and sixty-seven" do not enter any commas or periods (e.g.1,234,567 or 1.234.567). Instead, put all the digits of the number together (1234567).
    • To enter $xy$ you will need to enter the string x*y into the input field.
    • To enter a fraction such as $\frac pq$ you will need to enter the string p/q into the input field.
    • To enter $\sqrt{ax+b}$ you will need to enter the string sqrt(a*x+b) into the input field.
    • To enter $\sec^2 \left(ax+b\right)$ you will need to enter the string (sec(a*x+b))**2 or (sec(a*x+b))^2 into the input field.
    You may try out the MathLex language here.
  • You are not permitted to refer to any assisting websites or hard-copy documents. The following is a non-exhaustive list of some of the things you are not permitted to refer to:
    • An algebraic manipulation tool
    • A hard-copy formula sheet for differentiation and integration
    • A Computer Algebra System (CAS) or a Symbolic Algebra System (SAS)
    • A calculator which has symbolic differentiation and symbolic integration abilities
  • Answers which are not exact are to be rounded to 3 decimal places. You should round all values which 4th decimal place is more than or equal to 5 up. This means that you should round 1.23456 to 1.235 and round 6.54321 to 6.543. Do not follow the banker's rounding system which Python uses.
    • Exception: For question 4 leave your answer in exact form.
    • Exception: For question 7 leave the coefficient of your answer in exact form. This includes the term which does not include an $x$ in it (i.e. the term independent of $x$).
    • Exception: For question 10 and question 12 all coefficients of $x$ are to be in exact form.
  • A (scientific) calculator is to be used, whenever it is appropriate to do so. The calculator should have the following minimum features:
    1. Four arithmetic operations ($+$, $-$, $\times$, $\div$) including the order of operations and use of brackets/parentheses
    2. Fractional function which retains the fraction in the form $\frac pq$ where $p$ and $q$ are co-prime integers
    3. Square root ($\sqrt{\ }$) function
    4. Able to handle computations such as $p^q$ where $p$ is a real number and $q$ is a rational number
    5. Able to compute the binomial coefficient $n \choose r$
    It should not, however, have any of the following features:
    • Symbolic differentiation and symbolic integration abilities
  • Only the final answer for each question will be checked. Rough working for each question is optional.
    • However, it is recommended to prepare a blank sheet of A4 or US Letter paper to do some rough working for the more difficult questions.
  • Please ensure that audio is not disabled on this site, as music will be played during the duration of The Challenge.
